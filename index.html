Computer Look-Inside

  • CPU (Central Processing Unit)
    The "Brain" of the computer. Handles tasks and directs the other parts of the system.
  • Power Supply Unity
    This powers everything inside the computer. Near the Power Supply Unit, you find a spot to plug it into an outlet.
  • Motherboard
    The components of the computer are held on the motherboard. It allows components to communicate with eachother.
  • GPU (Graphic Processing Unit)
    The GPU connects to the monitors and displays visible information to us. When you hear someone say, "That game has terrible graphics," it may be because the GPU in the system isn't very powerful.
  • RAM (Random Access Memory)
    The RAM temporarily stores information for the computer to access quickly. Ram is volatile, meaning that the information on it will be overwritten eventually.
  • Hard Drive
    The hard drive stores the computer's data. All your files and software are stored on drives.
